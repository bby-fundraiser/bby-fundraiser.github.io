Frankford Yellow Jackets photo

The 1926 Frankford Yellow Jackets were the first NFL championship team in Philadelphia.  The Yellow Jackets were part of the National Football League from 1924 to 1931, although the team’s origin dates back to as early as 1899 with the Frankford Athletic Association. The team played its home games from 1923 in Frankford Stadium (also called Yellow Jacket Field) in Frankford, a neighborhood in the northeast of Philadelphia, noted for the Market–Frankford Line that terminates there. In 1926 they defeated the Chicago Bears at Shibe Park to win the championship.  Shibe Park was later renamed Connie Mack Stadium.
